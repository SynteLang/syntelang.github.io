Appreciation Thread

ok, you got me, this isn’t a thread at all but a blog post…
Well, anyway, I wanted to list some people and express gratitude for who they are.

Let’s begin…

thanks yaxu

I’ve said this before but probably the person I owe most to in live coding is Alex McLean (pronounced mclain or if you’re gaelic speaking something else).
Alex has put a lot of energy into putting on events, rallying support, building community, creating and maintaining TidalCycles which is used so widely in the UK (alongside its twin Strudel).
Although there have been many people involved in live coding going back decades, it’s hard to imagine the scene as it is today without Alex.
TOPLAP and social.toplap.org too.

Heavy Lifting

An appropriate artist name for sure, Lucy is a kind, sweet and humble person who has been extremely helpful and supportive of my journey into live coding and running events. Also brings awesome energy and ideas to events. She refuses to acknowledge this but I wouldn’t have done half or even any of the events I’ve taken the lead on here in London without Lucy’s support

Todepond

I can remember seeing this person online and I was like who is this person? Then I saw the Clojure talk and was like, oh right, cool!
Then Lu wrote a blog post called “I went to an algorave and it changed me forever” - an algorave that I led into being (I promise this post isn’t about my ego - the context is that I was really happy to see the impact the event made)
Then Lu came to a meetup and jumped right in - ‘getting it’ straight away.
Lu has infectious enthusiasm, a touch of mischief and does a lot to help live coding thrive in London. Like a LOT. Also has done a lot for collaborative jamming via the flok fork nudel.cc and the unknowable phenomenon #pastagang.

mah[alias]

Mahalia was the first person to help me get a foothold in the London scene, by chatting to me at a Corsica Algorave (two in fact) and listening to my ramblings as well as encouraging me to get involved. She also has guest hosted a meetup and did a great job. As well as being a lovely person and hugely talented.

hellocatfood

Antonio has done all the posters for our algoraves at Corsica studios, and brings awesome energy to performance as well as being the busiest live coder in the uk. And deservedly so. Antonio always brings others up, most notably through leading the (Algo|Afro) Futures mentoring programme, which has spawned a lot of great talent into the scene.

monz

Stephen gave lots of advice and support which helped in running algoraves, also has put a lot of time, energy and money into running fake[dac~] events. Stephen can be a pain sometimes hehe but always brings lots of ideas and passion. Also helpfully created the algorave_london insta.

Corsica Studios and The Carpet Shop

The lovely people who run both of these venues have been very supportive and all the staff are extremely lovely. It can feel like a family being part of the events.
